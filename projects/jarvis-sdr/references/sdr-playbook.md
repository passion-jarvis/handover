# Jarvis SDR Playbook — Phone Call Scripts

George's call framework for job-signal leads. Every call starts the same. The close adapts to the keyword category.

---

## Call Framework (All Categories)

### Before Every Call
- Check GHL note: what role they're hiring for, company size, revenue estimate, score
- Pull their LinkedIn: know their industry, how long they've been the owner
- Know the pain: "they're hiring [X] which means they're drowning in [specific task]"
- Rate yourself after: script, tonality, body language, rapport, energy (1–5 each)

### Opening (first 10 seconds)
> "Hey [Name], this is George from Jarvis — we help business owners replace hiring headaches with pre-trained virtual assistants. I noticed [Company] is hiring a [job title] — is that still something you're working on?"

**If yes:** "Perfect. I've got 2 minutes — can I show you why most people who hire [that role] end up doing it twice?"

**If no / filled:** "Got it — does that mean the problem is solved, or just paused?"

**If gatekeeper:** "I'm reaching out to [Name] about a virtual assistant solution for the [role] they're hiring. Is [Name] available or should I send a quick email first?"

### Qualifying Questions (pick 2-3, don't interrogate)
1. "How long has that role been open?"
2. "What's taking most of [Name]'s time right now that a hire would fix?"
3. "Have you worked with VAs before — or is this your first time?"
4. "What's the timeline — is this urgent or planning ahead?"
5. "What's your hesitation with a VA versus a full-time hire?"

### The Bridge (mid-call pivot)
> "Here's what we see every time: business owners hire [role] because they're overwhelmed. But the hire takes 6–8 weeks, costs $50K+ a year, and 40% of the time it's the wrong fit. We solve that in 5 business days for $1,600/month — and if it's not right, we replace them. No questions."

### The Ask
> "I want to set you up with a 20-minute strategy call with our founder. Not a sales call — she'll map exactly what a VA could take off your plate this week. Can we find 20 minutes?"

**If push back on time:** "I respect your time. Can I send you a 3-minute overview and we pick up from there?"

## Keyword-Specific Scripts

### EA / Executive Assistant (`keyword_category: ea`)

**Pain statement:**
> "You're hiring an EA which tells me your calendar, inbox, and ops are running you instead of the other way around. That's the most expensive problem a CEO can have."

**Hook:**
> "Our EA-trained VAs do exactly what an in-house EA does — scheduling, inbox zero, travel, follow-ups — but they're already trained. You don't have to teach them anything. They start in 5 days."

**Objection: "I need someone in-person"**
> "I hear you. Can I challenge that? 90% of what an EA does doesn't need them in the room. Your last EA — what did they actually do in person that couldn't be done remotely?"

**Objection: "I tried a VA before and it didn't work"**
> "That's the most common thing I hear. The difference: most VA services give you a generalist. We give you an EA specialist — pre-trained on your type of business, monitored by a Jarvis success manager. What failed last time?"

---

### Social Media Hire (`keyword_category: social`)

**Pain statement:**
> "You're hiring a social media person, which means content is either not going out or it's all on you. Either way, the business isn't showing up online the way it should."

**Hook:**
> "Our social VAs handle content scheduling, caption writing, comment management, and reporting. They work in Creator Studio, Later, Buffer — whatever you're using. And they're $1,600/month versus $4K+/month for a US-based hire."

**Objection: "Content has to be authentic — can a VA really do that?"**
> "Fair question. They don't create strategy — you do, or we help you build the framework. But execution? Scheduling 30 posts a month, writing captions from your notes, repurposing your content? That's 100% VA territory. Most of our clients give us a voice guide and they're hands-off within 2 weeks."

**Objection: "I need someone who understands my brand"**
> "That's exactly what our onboarding SOP builds. We document your brand voice, your content pillars, your aesthetic — before the VA starts. By day 5, they're posting in your voice."

---

### BDR / Business Development Rep (`keyword_category: bdr`)

**Pain statement:**
> "You're hiring a BDR — which means either outbound is broken, leads are slipping, or you're personally doing prospecting you shouldn't be doing."

**Hook:**
> "Our BDR-trained VAs handle Apollo sequences, LinkedIn outreach, follow-up DMs, and pipeline updates in your CRM. Everything except the actual sales call. They're the engine — you or your closer is the closer."

**Objection: "Sales outreach has to be personalized — a VA can't do that"**
> "They're not sending generic blasts. They work from your ICP, your message templates, and they research each lead before outreach. Think of them as a prospecting engine, not a spammer."

**Objection: "We use [HubSpot/Salesforce] — can they work in that?"**
> "Yes. We onboard VAs into your existing stack — HubSpot, Salesforce, GHL, whatever. They're trained on the tool, not just the concept."

---

### Sales Hire (`keyword_category: sales`)

**Pain statement:**
> "You're hiring a salesperson — which usually means the pipeline is strong but the follow-up is broken, or you're doing sales and ops at the same time."

**Hook:**
> "We can't replace your closer. But we can give them a VA that handles everything around the close: booking, prep notes, follow-up emails, CRM updates, proposal delivery. Your closer closes more because admin disappears."

**Reframe (if they want a full sales VA):**
> "I want to be straight with you — we're not a sales agency. But the #1 reason salespeople underperform is they spend 40% of their time on non-selling tasks. What if we removed that 40%?"

**Objection: "We need someone who can sell, not just admin"**
> "Then let's talk about what specifically you need them to do. If it's prospecting and outbound, that's one thing. If it's closing, that's a different hire. Help me understand what the revenue gap actually is."

---

### Video Editor Hire (`keyword_category: video`)

**Pain statement:**
> "You're hiring a video editor — which means content is backing up, turnaround is slow, or you're paying US rates for editing work that doesn't need to cost that much."

**Hook:**
> "Our video editing VAs work in Premiere, CapCut, DaVinci, Final Cut — whatever your stack is. Reels, YouTube longform, ads, testimonials. They turn around within 24–48 hours. For $1,600/month, most clients get 15–20 videos edited per month."

**Objection: "My brand has a specific style — they won't get it"**
> "Send them 3 reference videos and a style brief. By edit 3, you'll stop giving feedback. That's the pattern we see every time."

**Objection: "I need someone available in my timezone"**
> "Our VAs work overlap hours — 9 AM to 2 PM your time, minimum. For video editing specifically, most of the work is async anyway. You drop files, they deliver. Do you need real-time collaboration or just fast turnaround?"

## Common Objections (All Categories)

### "It's too expensive"
> "The price or the cost of not solving this? You're either paying $1,600 for a trained system, or you're paying in your own time, a bad hire, and a 3-month ramp. Which one are you willing to pay?"

### "I need to think about it"
> "What specifically do you need to think through? Is it budget, whether it'll work, or something else? I want to give you the right information before we hang up."

### "Can you send me more info?"
> "I can. But info doesn't answer your real question, which is: will this work for my business? That's what the strategy call is for. It's 20 minutes. If it's not a fit, I'll tell you."

### "We're not ready yet"
> "What needs to happen before you'd be ready? I'm asking because most people who say 'not yet' are ready — they just don't know what the first step looks like. Let me show you that."

### "I had a bad experience with VAs"
> "Tell me what happened. Because if Jarvis has that same problem, I'd rather know now than waste your time. And if we don't — you deserve to hear why."

## Scoring Yourself After Every Call

Rate 1–5 on each:
- **Script:** Did I hit all the key points without sounding scripted?
- **Tonality:** Was I confident, calm, not pitchy?
- **Rapport:** Did they warm up? Did I match their energy?
- **Objection handling:** Did I address each objection before they gave up?
- **The ask:** Did I get a clear yes/no/next step?

**Total 20+ = strong call. Under 15 = review the recording.**

## Notes for George

- Stay silent after stating the investment. Whoever speaks first loses.
- It's you + client vs. the objection. Never you vs. the client.
- The real sale is done in the middle of the call — not the close.
- If they say no at the end: "What would need to be different for this to be a yes?"
- If they book but ghost: call 3x in 5 days. Third call: "I'm going to close out your file — is there a reason it didn't work out?"
