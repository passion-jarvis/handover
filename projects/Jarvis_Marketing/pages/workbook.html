Before You Begin
There Are Two Types
of Business Owner.
Type A — The Operator

Knows every client. Approves every email. Checks every invoice. The business depends on them for everything. Revenue grows — but so does the weight. They feel indispensable, but really, they're trapped.

They don't have a business. They have a job — with more paperwork and no days off.

Type B — The CEO

Has a team. Has systems. Has a pipeline that works whether they're on the clock or not. Their job is to make decisions — not complete tasks. Revenue grows because they built something that can grow without them.

They have a business. One that can one day run — or sell — without them.

The Question That Changes Everything

"If I disappeared for 30 days, what would break?"

Your honest answer tells you exactly how much work this workbook has to do. Most owners who answer this question realize they've been building a cage, not a company.

This workbook is about making the shift from Type A to Type B. It's not complicated. But it does require you to let go of the belief that only you can do it right.

Ready? Let's start with your time.

The 1-3-1 Delegation Rule
When handing off any task: give 1 clear outcome, 3 checkpoints to verify progress, and 1 decision they own completely. This eliminates the "did you check in yet?" loop.
