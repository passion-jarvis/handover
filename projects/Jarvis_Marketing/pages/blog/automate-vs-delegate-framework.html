What to Automate vs What to Delegate: The Exact Decision Framework

April 22, 20269 min readBy Jarvis VA

Every task in your business belongs in one of three buckets: automate it, delegate it, or do it yourself. Most founders have no system for deciding which is which. Here's the framework we use with every Jarvis client on day one.

Why this decision matters more than you think

When founders first come to Jarvis, they usually have one of two problems. Either they're trying to automate everything with AI and it keeps failing on them — customers getting bot-replies to real complaints, leads going cold because no human followed up. Or they're delegating tasks to a VA that could be handled by a $10/month automation tool, which means they're paying human rates for robotic work.

Both are expensive mistakes. The first costs you in customer trust and conversion. The second costs you in unnecessary payroll. The fix is a clear mental model for sorting tasks before you assign them anywhere.

Ask one question about every task:
"Does this require judgment, relationships, or context — or just execution?"

The two-question test

Before you decide where any task goes, run it through these two questions:

Question 1: Is the output always the same if the input is the same?

If someone orders the wrong item and wants a refund, should the response always be identical? No — it depends on the customer's history, their tone, how many times this has happened, and whether saving the relationship matters more than the refund. That's a VA task.

If a new lead fills out a form and needs a confirmation email sent, should the output always be identical? Yes. That's automation.

Question 2: Would a mistake cost you a customer or just a task?

Automation mistakes are cheap when they're low-stakes. An invoice gets sent with the wrong line item — easy to fix, no relationship damage. But if AI sends a tone-deaf response to an upset client and they screenshot it? That's a reputation problem. High-stakes communication belongs with a human.

The pattern you'll notice

Look at the VA column. Almost every task in it has one of three things in common: it involves a real relationship, it requires reading context the system doesn't have, or a mistake would cost more than money.

Look at the automation column. Almost every task in it is rule-based, templated, or time-triggered. The machine knows exactly what to do because you've already decided what the right output is.

Automation is decision execution. A VA is decision making. Once you see it that way, sorting your task list becomes obvious.

How Jarvis combines both

Every Jarvis client gets both layers. We use Durable — an automation platform with 50+ integrations — to handle all the rule-based execution: routing, triggering, logging, scheduling. Your VA handles everything that requires judgment, context, and relationship.

In practice, a task often flows through both. A new lead comes in. Durable auto-tags them by source and triggers an alert to the VA. The VA responds within 2 minutes in the founder's voice, qualifies the lead, and books the call. Durable logs the outcome and triggers the onboarding sequence when the deal closes. The founder never touches it.

The goal isn't to replace human judgment with automation. It's to clear away everything that doesn't need judgment so your VA — and you — can focus on the work that actually moves the business.

Where to start

Take 20 minutes this week and list every task you or your team does in a day. For each one, ask: "Does this require a real decision, or is it always the same if the input is the same?" Sort them into two columns. The automation column becomes your Durable setup. The VA column becomes your day-one hire brief.

That's the exercise we walk through with every new Jarvis client on the first call. Most founders are surprised how many tasks fall into automation — and how much more clearly they can see what they actually need a VA for.
