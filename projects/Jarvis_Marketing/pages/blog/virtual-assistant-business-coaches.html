The problem no one talks about: the leads you already have

Most coaches focus their energy on getting more leads. Better content, better ads, better hooks. But the conversion problem isn't usually at the top of the funnel. It's in the middle — where leads land in your DMs or inbox, and then nothing happens.

Business coaches are creators first. You're recording content, delivering sessions, building curriculum, managing your own clients. The DMs pile up fast. By the time you get to them, the prospect has already moved on — signed with another coach, bought a course, or just lost momentum.

The leads aren't the problem.
The unanswered messages are.

The second use case: bookkeeping

Business coaches often carry a second operational burden that rarely gets talked about: the books. You're running a service business with multiple income streams — 1:1 clients, group programs, courses, speaking. Every month there's invoicing, expense tracking, and a P&L your accountant is waiting for.

We worked with another business coach doing $30K a month who was spending 6 hours every week on bookkeeping. Not because he was bad at it — he just had nobody else to do it. His VA now handles everything: categorizes expenses, sends invoices, tracks payments, chases late ones, and prepares a monthly summary. He gets 6 hours back every week. Sunday nights are his again.

Which type of VA do you need first?

It depends on where you're bleeding most. If you have leads coming in and they're going cold in your DMs, the DM manager is the highest-ROI hire — the return in month one usually covers the cost for the rest of the year. If you're already converting well but drowning in operations, start with an operations VA who takes everything non-coaching off your plate.

Most coaches end up with both, at different times. The first hire clears the biggest bottleneck. The second one multiplies your capacity.
