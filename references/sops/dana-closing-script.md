## The Jarvis Offer (Know This Cold)

### The Problem We Solve (Say This In Your Head Before Every Call)

Many capable founders are trapped in their own business. They don't trust others to take over, so they end up doing everything themselves. As the business grows, the workload increases — and eventually, growth slows down because THEY become the bottleneck.

That's who you're calling. They built something real. But they're drowning in the day-to-day and can't get out.

**What you're selling isn't a VA. You're selling the founder's life back** — time with family, thinking strategically, actually enjoying what they built.

---

### What Jarvis Does

We help established founders automate and delegate their daily operations so they can focus on what actually grows the business.

**Target client:** Online business owners (e-commerce), doing $10K+/month, US and Australia.

---

### The 3 Uniques (What Makes Us Different)

**Unique #1 — VA + Automations, not just VA:**
Your VA does the work. Then automates it so it runs without them. Jarvis VAs handle your operations AND build the automations that eliminate the repetitive work. Powered by AI. No developer needed.

**Unique #2 — 200:1 selection ratio:**
We train and filter VAs at a 200:1 ratio. You get someone pre-trained, vetted, and ready to start from Day 1. You don't train them — we do.

**Unique #3 — No payment until ready:**
You don't pay a single dollar until your VA is fully trained and ready to start working in your business. Zero risk on your end.

---

1. AI-trained VA, matched to their business type
2. Pre-trained before Day 1 — hits the ground running

4. Unlimited replacement — if it's not the right fit, we replace for free, no limits

## Call Structure

### PHASE 1 — OPENER (First 15 seconds)
_Goal: Get them to stay on the line._

**For Meta leads (inbound):**
> "Hey [Name], this is Dana calling from Jarvis — you filled out a form on our ad recently. Did I catch you at a bad time?"

If they say yes / busy:
> "No worries at all — when's a better time? I'll call you then."
_(Book the callback. Don't push.)_

If they say no / go ahead:
> "Perfect. So you were looking into getting some help with your business — can you tell me a little about what you're working on?"

**For outbound leads (Apollo):**
> "Hey [Name], this is Dana from Jarvis. I know you weren't expecting my call — I'll keep it quick. You run [Company], right? We help business owners like you get back 20+ hours a week by putting a trained VA on your operations. Is that something worth a 2-minute conversation?"

---

### PHASE 2 — DISCOVERY (80% of the call is them talking)
_Goal: Find their pain. Their pain closes the deal — not your pitch._

Ask these questions. Listen more than you talk.

> "Tell me about your business — what do you sell and how long have you been running it?"

> "What does your week actually look like right now? Where are you spending most of your time?"

> "What's taking the most time that you wish you didn't have to deal with?"

> "Have you ever tried delegating before? What happened?"

> "If you got 20 hours back every week — what would you actually do with that time?"

**Listen for:** email, customer service, scheduling, admin, follow-ups, invoicing, data entry, social media, lead response. These are all VA tasks.

**Key rule:** The sale is done in the middle. By the time you're halfway through discovery, they should be sold — if you've done it right.

---

### PHASE 3 — PRESENT THE SOLUTION
_Goal: Connect what they told you to what Jarvis delivers. Use their own words._

> "Okay, so from what you've told me — you're spending [X hours] on [specific tasks they mentioned], and that's pulling you away from [what they said they want to focus on]. That's exactly who we built Jarvis for."

> "Here's what we do: we match you with a VA who's already pre-trained for your type of business. They start handling [tasks they mentioned] from Week 1. And on top of that, they build out automations so the repetitive stuff just runs itself."

> "What most of our clients say is that within the first 30 days, they get back 15-20 hours a week. What would that mean for you?"

_(Let them answer. That answer is the close.)_

---

### PHASE 4 — INVESTMENT
_Always say "investment," never "price" or "cost."_

> "The investment is $1,600 a month. That's roughly $10 an hour — for someone who works your hours, knows your business, and builds the systems that keep it running."

**Then stop. Say nothing. Whoever speaks first after the investment amount loses.**

If they pause and say nothing, wait. Hold the silence.

---

### PHASE 5 — OBJECTION HANDLING

**Rule for every objection:** "I hear you. Can I challenge that?"

Then use logic + emotion together. Never argue. It's YOU + THEM vs the OBJECTION.

---

**"It's too expensive."**
> "I hear you. Can I challenge that? The question isn't whether $1,600 is expensive — it's whether the cost of NOT doing this is higher. You're spending [X hours/week] on tasks you just described. At your revenue level, your time is worth $[X] an hour. You're paying anyway — just with your time instead of money. Which one are you more willing to pay?"

---

**"I need to think about it."**
> "I hear you. Can I challenge that? What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, whether it'll work for your business, or something else?"

_(Get them to name it. Then handle that specific objection. "Need to think about it" is never the real objection.)_

---

**"I've tried VAs before and it didn't work."**
> "I hear you — and honestly, most VAs don't work. That's why we built Jarvis differently. We put them through a 200:1 selection process, pre-train them before they touch your business, and if they're not right, we replace them for free. The reason past VAs failed wasn't you — it was that no one set them up properly. We do that part."

---

**"I don't have time to train someone."**
> "That's actually the reason you need this. You're too busy because you don't have help — and you can't get help because you're too busy. It's a loop. Our VAs come pre-trained. Day 1, they know what to do. You don't train them — we do."

---

**"Can I get a trial first?"**
> "We actually have a guarantee built in — you don't pay until your VA is fully trained and ready to start. And if it's not the right fit, we replace them for free, as many times as it takes. You're not taking a risk. We are."

---

**"I need to talk to my partner / spouse."**
> "Totally makes sense. Quick question — if your partner said yes today, would you move forward? Or is there something you yourself still need to feel comfortable with?"

_(If they say "yes, if my partner agrees" — offer to schedule a joint call. Don't let them disappear.)_

---

**"I'm not sure I make enough to justify it."**
> "You mentioned you're doing [X] a month. At that revenue level, your time is your biggest asset. If hiring a VA helps you close one more client a month, or hold onto the ones you have — that pays for itself immediately. What would one more retained client mean for you?"

---

### PHASE 6 — CLOSE

When they feel good and objections are resolved:

> "Okay — so based on everything you've told me, this makes sense. Here's what I want to do: let's get you started. I'll send you a quick intake form right now — takes 5 minutes — and we'll have your VA matched and ready within the week. Does that work?"

If they're ready to go:
> "Perfect. What's the best email to send the onboarding link to?"

If they want a follow-up call first:
> "No problem. Let's lock in a 20-minute call so I can walk you through exactly what your VA would handle and answer any remaining questions. What does your calendar look like this week?"

**Book the follow-up call with YOURSELF — not Passion. You close on that call.**

On the follow-up call, start with:
> "Last time we talked, you mentioned [specific pain]. I want to make sure we solve that. Here's exactly how your VA would handle it from Week 1..."

Then go straight to the investment and close.

---

### PHASE 7 — CONFIRM THE DEAL

When they say yes:

> "Perfect. I'm sending you the onboarding link to [email] right now. You'll see a short form — fill it out today so we can start the matching process. Your VA will be ready within the week."

Confirm:
- Their email for the onboarding link
- Best phone number on file
- Any specific tasks they want handled from Day 1

> "One last thing — do you have any questions before I send this over?"

## After the Call — Rate Yourself

Score yourself 1-10 on each:
- Script — did you follow the flow?
- Energy — were you genuinely excited about what Jarvis does?
- Tonality — did you sound confident without being pushy?
- Rapport — did they open up and talk?
- Objections — did you handle or avoid them?

If any score is below 7, write down what to improve before the next call.

---

## Key Rules (Non-Negotiable)

- Never say "price." Always say "investment."
- After you say the investment amount — shut up.
- A great call is 80% them talking, 20% you.
- Never argue with an objection. Agree, then challenge it.
- Your job = close the deal on the call. If not today, book a follow-up with yourself to close.
- Never hand off to Passion — you are the closer.
- Log every call in GHL within 5 minutes of hanging up.
