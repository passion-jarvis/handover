# INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT AND NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Independent Contractor Agreement and Non-Disclosure Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of April 7, 2026 ("Effective Date") by and between:

**Company:** Jarvis (gojarvis.ai), a business operated by Passion Chu, with principal place of business in Los Angeles, California ("Company" or "Jarvis")

**Contractor:** Dana Alfarouk ("Contractor")

Each individually a "Party" and together the "Parties."

## 1. ENGAGEMENT AND ROLE

**1.1** Company engages Contractor as an independent contractor in the role of Closer ("Closer"). Contractor's primary responsibility is to conduct sales calls with Company-provided leads, present the Jarvis offer, and close new clients into a paid engagement ("Closed Deal").

**1.2 Definition of "Closed Deal."** A "Closed Deal" means a prospect who satisfies all of the following conditions as determined by Company in its reasonable discretion:

(a) Was sourced exclusively from Company-provided leads and introduced to Contractor through Company's CRM or other Company-designated channel;

(b) Attended a sales call conducted by Contractor and was actively advanced by Contractor through the sales process;

(c) Executed a written client agreement with Jarvis in the form approved by Company, without material modification;

(d) Made their first payment to Company in the full amount required by the client agreement, and that payment was successfully collected and cleared; and

(e) Was accepted by Company as a client in its sole discretion, including with respect to suitability, compliance, and fit with Company's service model.

For the avoidance of doubt: a deal does not qualify if the prospect signed but payment was declined, reversed, or refunded; if Company elected not to onboard the prospect for any reason; or if Contractor's involvement was not the proximate cause of the closing as reasonably determined by Company. Company's determination of whether a deal qualifies as a Closed Deal is final and binding, subject only to the written dispute process in Section 3.4.

**1.3** Contractor agrees to perform Services professionally, accurately represent the Company's offer, and comply with all Company-approved scripts, pricing, and sales guidelines in all prospect-facing interactions.

## 2. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

**2.1 No Employment Relationship.** Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of Company. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment relationship. Company will not withhold income taxes, Social Security, Medicare, or any other employment taxes on Contractor's behalf. Contractor is solely responsible for all taxes on compensation received under this Agreement and will receive a Form 1099 if payments exceed $600 in a calendar year.

**2.2 Control Over Work.** Contractor controls the manner, means, and methods by which Services are performed, subject to the output requirements in this Agreement. Contractor sets their own schedule and hours. Company may provide approved scripts, email templates, and CRM tools as resources — use of these resources is encouraged but Contractor retains discretion in how Services are performed within the bounds of Company guidelines.

**2.3 Tools and Equipment.** Contractor will use their own devices, phone, and internet connection to perform Services. Company will provide access to its CRM platform (GoHighLevel), a Company email address, and approved call scripts as resources.

**2.4 Other Clients.** Contractor may work with other clients during the term of this Agreement, provided those clients are not direct competitors of Jarvis. A "direct competitor" means any business that provides virtual assistant staffing, VA placement, VA matching, or any substantially similar service. Contractor must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to Company in writing before taking on any new client relationship.

**2.5 No Benefits.** Contractor is not entitled to any employee benefits, including health insurance, paid time off, workers' compensation, unemployment insurance, or retirement benefits.

**2.6 Classification Acknowledgment.** Both Parties intend this to be an independent contractor relationship. Both Parties acknowledge that worker classification under California law (Labor Code Section 2775 et seq.) is determined by law and not solely by the terms of this Agreement.

## 3. COMPENSATION

**3.1 Commission Structure.** Contractor will be paid on the following commission-only basis. No base pay, retainer, draw, or hourly rate is provided or implied.

- **Per Closed Deal:** $300.00 USD per Closed Deal
- **Monthly Recurring Bonus:** 10% of monthly revenue attributable to each retained client introduced by Contractor, paid monthly for as long as that client remains active, subject to Company's right to discontinue recurring commissions under Section 3.6

**3.2 Payment Schedule.** Commissions earned in a given calendar month will be paid on the 15th day of the following month (or the next business day if the 15th falls on a weekend or holiday), provided the underlying client payment has been successfully collected and cleared. Recurring bonuses are paid on the same schedule, based on confirmed active client status at month end as determined by Company's records.

**3.3 Clawback Policy.** Commissions paid to Contractor are subject to the following clawbacks:

(a) Cancellation or non-payment within 90 days of the client's first payment: full $300.00 per-deal commission is clawed back in its entirety;

(b) Cancellation between days 91 and 120: $150.00 (50%) clawback applies;

(c) Cancellation after 120 days: no clawback on the per-deal commission; it is fully earned;

(d) Recurring bonuses are not earned and will not be paid for any month in which the client is not active, has paused, or is in arrears on payment to Company;

(e) If a client's payment is reversed, charged back, or refunded for any reason, any commission paid in connection with that client's first payment is subject to full clawback regardless of timing.

Company will provide written notice of any clawback within 10 business days of the triggering event. If clawback amounts exceed Contractor's pending commissions, Contractor agrees to pay the outstanding difference to Company within 15 days of written notice. Unpaid clawback amounts bear interest at 1.5% per month until paid.

**3.4 Disputed Commissions.** If Contractor disputes a commission determination or clawback, Contractor must raise the dispute in writing within 5 business days of receiving the relevant payout summary or clawback notice, specifying the basis for the dispute in detail. Company will review and provide a written response within 7 business days. Contractor's failure to raise a dispute within the 5-business-day window constitutes a waiver of that claim.

**3.5 Non-Qualifying Deals.** Prospects who do not satisfy every element of the Closed Deal definition in Section 1.2 do not qualify for any commission. Submission of a deal for commission does not create an obligation on Company's part to pay unless and until Company confirms qualification.

**3.6 Discontinuation of Recurring Commissions.** Company may discontinue Contractor's eligibility for monthly recurring bonuses on any or all clients by providing 30 days written notice to Contractor. Upon discontinuation, Contractor will continue to receive recurring bonuses through the end of the 30-day notice period for clients who remain active, after which no further recurring bonuses will accrue.

**3.7 Forfeiture Upon Termination for Cause.** If this Agreement is terminated by Company for cause under Section 10.3, Contractor forfeits all unpaid commissions and recurring bonuses that have accrued but not yet been paid as of the termination date, and Company has no further payment obligation to Contractor. Contractor acknowledges this forfeiture provision as a material term of this Agreement.

## 4. PERFORMANCE MINIMUMS

**4.1 Month 1 KPI.** During the first 30 days of this Agreement, Contractor must close a minimum of 2 Closed Deals. If this minimum is not met, Company may terminate this Agreement immediately with no notice and no further compensation obligation beyond commissions earned for Closed Deals that fully qualified prior to termination.

**4.2 Ongoing Performance.** After Month 1, Company may set, communicate, and adjust performance expectations through written notice (including via email or WhatsApp message). Consistent failure to meet agreed performance targets for two or more consecutive weeks is grounds for immediate termination under Section 10.3.

## 5. LEAD DATA AND CRM ACCESS

**5.1 Proprietary Leads.** All leads provided by Company are the sole and exclusive proprietary assets and confidential business information of Jarvis. Lead data includes, without limitation, names, phone numbers, email addresses, business names, revenue data, conversation history, notes, tags, and any other data stored in or derived from Company's CRM. Contractor acquires no ownership, license, or interest in any lead data by virtue of this Agreement.

**5.2 Authorized Use Only.** Contractor is authorized to access lead data exclusively through Company's CRM platform (GoHighLevel) and solely for the purpose of performing Services under this Agreement. Contractor is strictly prohibited from:

(a) Exporting, copying, downloading, printing, or screenshotting any lead data in any format;

(b) Saving or storing lead data to personal devices, personal email accounts, spreadsheets, note-taking applications, messaging platforms, or any third-party tool not owned by Company;

(c) Sharing, transmitting, or disclosing lead data to any person or entity not authorized by Company in writing;

(d) Using lead data for any purpose other than the Services under this Agreement; and

(e) Retaining any lead data, in any format or medium, after termination of this Agreement for any reason.

**5.3 Liquidated Damages for Lead Data Breach.** Both Parties agree that actual damages from unauthorized use, copying, retention, or disclosure of lead data would be difficult to calculate, given the costs of lead acquisition through paid advertising and the long-term revenue value of each client relationship. Contractor therefore agrees to pay $500.00 per lead improperly accessed, copied, retained, used, or disclosed as liquidated damages — not as a penalty — representing a reasonable estimate of Company's minimum harm per incident. This provision does not limit Company's right to seek injunctive relief under Section 6 or to pursue claims for damages exceeding this amount where actual harm can be demonstrated.

**5.4 Return and Deletion.** Within 24 hours of termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason, Contractor must permanently delete all Company materials, lead data, client information, scripts, and any other Company Confidential Information stored outside of Company-owned systems. Contractor must confirm such deletion in writing to passion@gojarvis.ai within the same 24-hour period.

**6.4 No Limitation on Remedies.** The right to seek injunctive relief is in addition to, and not in lieu of, any other remedies available to Company at law or in equity.

## 10. TERM AND TERMINATION

**10.1 Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until terminated by either Party.

**10.2 Termination with Notice.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement by providing 7 days written notice via email. Contractor's obligations under Sections 5, 6, 7, 11, 12, and 13 survive termination regardless of which Party initiates it.

**10.3 Immediate Termination for Cause.** Company may terminate this Agreement immediately, without notice or further payment obligation, upon:

(a) Contractor's failure to meet the Month 1 minimum of 2 Closed Deals (Section 4.1);

(b) Contractor's failure to meet ongoing performance targets for two or more consecutive weeks (Section 4.2);

(c) Any breach of Section 5 (Lead Data) or Section 11 (Non-Disclosure);

(d) Any breach of Section 12 (Non-Solicitation);

(e) Dishonest, fraudulent, or grossly unprofessional conduct, including material misrepresentations to prospects;

(f) Use of personal communication channels in violation of Section 8.1; or

(g) Submission of fabricated or manipulated deal activity to obtain commissions.

**10.4 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination: (a) Contractor's right to earn future commissions and recurring bonuses ends immediately; (b) if terminated for cause under Section 10.3, Section 3.7 applies and all unpaid commissions are forfeited; (c) if terminated without cause, commissions already earned and not subject to clawback will be paid on the next regular payment date; (d) Contractor must comply with Section 5.4 within 24 hours; and (e) Contractor must comply with the cooperation obligations in Section 12.3 for up to 30 days following termination.
