_Jarvis | How to Find, Read, and Prioritize Leads_

## Where Your Leads Are

1. Open **GoHighLevel** → log in with your dana@gojarvis.ai credentials
2. Left sidebar → click **Contacts**
3. All your assigned leads are here

You only see leads that are assigned to you. Do not attempt to search or browse outside your assigned contacts.

---

## How to Read a Lead

When you click on a contact, here's what matters:

| Field | What it tells you |
|-------|------------------|
| **Name + Company** | Who they are, what business they run |
| **Phone** | Primary number to call — always call first |
| **Email** | Backup if no answer after 3 attempts |
| **Source** | Where they came from (Facebook = Meta ad, Apollo = outbound) |
| **Tags** | Status flags — read these before calling |
| **Notes** | Any previous contact history — read before dialing |
| **Date Added** | How fresh the lead is — freshness = higher intent |

---

## Who to Call First — Priority Order

**Call in this order, every single day:**

### 🔴 Priority 1 — Fresh Meta Leads (Source: Facebook, added today or yesterday)
These people just raised their hand on an ad. They're warm. Call within 24 hours or intent drops fast.
- Source = Facebook or Instagram
- Date added = today or yesterday
- Has a phone number

### 🟠 Priority 2 — Leads You've Already Spoken To (Status: Contacted / Follow-up)
Anyone you've already had a conversation with. They know who Jarvis is — easier to re-engage than a cold lead.
- Check the Notes section for what was discussed
- Pick up exactly where you left off — do not start over

### 🟡 Priority 3 — Older Meta Leads (Source: Facebook, 3-7 days old)
Still warmer than outbound. They saw the ad and filled out a form. Just been sitting.

### ⚪ Priority 4 — Apollo / Outbound Leads
These people did not ask to be contacted. Be sharp and get to the point fast. You have less than 15 seconds to hook them.

## Before Every Call — 60 Second Prep

1. Read their name, company name, and any notes
2. Google their business if you have 30 seconds — know what they sell
3. Know your opener before you dial (see closing script)
4. Have your calendar link ready to book if they're interested

---

## After Every Call — Log It Immediately

Every call must be logged in GHL before moving to the next lead:

- **Answered / Not Interested** → tag: Dead, add note with what they said
- **Answered / Follow-up needed** → tag: Follow-up, add note + set a task reminder
- **Answered / Booked** → tag: Booked, add the call date/time in notes
- **No answer** → tag: Attempted, note: "VM left" or "no answer — try again [date]"

If it's not logged, it didn't happen. Commission depends on accurate records.

## Booking a Call

When they say yes to learning more:

> "Perfect. Let me get you on a call with Passion, our founder — he does a quick 20-minute strategy session. What does your calendar look like this week?"

Use the Company calendar link to book (Passion will provide this). Do not give your personal number or email. All bookings go through the Company calendar.
