# Sales Philosophy

Passion's core sales beliefs. Apply when drafting sales copy, outreach scripts, coaching George/Shemily, or writing ad creative.

1. Sales is a game you're playing whether you like it or not. You're either selling or being sold.
2. Use both logic and emotion when selling.
3. Sales is 80% energy and mindset.
4. When people say No, they are saying No to the conversation, not you. They are saying No to their future selves.
5. Reward yourself on bad days.
6. No high energy output comes from low energy input.
7. Every person has their own personality and leverage point. Use that when selling.
8. Treat others how THEY want to be treated. Adjust your manner based on the prospect (young, old, etc).
9. The actual sale is done in the middle (80%), not at the beginning or the end.
10. Overcome each objection during the conversation before they mention them in the end.
11. A great sales call is 80% client talking, 20% you.
12. Don't use "PRICE" — use "INVESTMENT" instead.
13. When you say the investment amount, stay silent. Whoever speaks first after the price loses.
14. It's YOU + CLIENT vs OBJECTION. Not you against the client.
15. The sale is done when they feel good about saying yes.
16. For each objection say: "I hear you. Can I challenge that?"
17. In a prospect's mind there's an equation: (Outcome × Certainty) — (Cost × Risk). Increase the first part, decrease the second.
18. Bring them down to a realistic outcome if they set too big of one — otherwise they will feel unmotivated.
19. When they say "It's too expensive" ask: "The price or the cost of inaction? You pay anyway. Which one are you willing to pay?"
20. Be prepared for everything to go wrong when closing — card declined, connection drops, paperwork issues. Expect it.
21. Practice your pitch before every call and rate yourself: script, tonality, body language, rapport, energy.
22. You're not selling — you're exchanging value for money.
