## The Sales Call Framework

### 3 Phases

**PHASE 1: OPENING (10% of call)**  
Guard is HIGH. Goal: establish authority, get them to relax.

**PHASE 2: DISCOVERY (80% of call)**  
Guard LOWERS. This is where the sale actually happens — before you ever show price.

**PHASE 3: CLOSE + OBJECTIONS (10% of call)**  
Guard goes BACK UP when price comes. Goal: handle what's left and get the commitment.

---

## Phase 1: Opening (First 2 Minutes)

**Rule: State your purpose BEFORE building rapport. Never the other way around.**

Rapport before purpose = they smell the sales frame and go defensive.  
Purpose first → rapport = genuine, lowers guard.

**Script:**
> "Hey [name], nice to meet you. So you filled out our form about getting VA support — that's exactly what I help with. We're working with a lot of business owners right now who are in the same spot you are. Super pumped to learn more about what you've got going on. Is it cool if we just jump right in?"

**Tonality:**
- End every sentence as a STATEMENT, not a question
- Downward inflection = confident
- Upward inflection = unsure → kills authority immediately

---

## Phase 2: Discovery — The 3-Bucket Framework

**Your goal: get them talking 80% of the time. You talk 20%.**

Think of yourself as a doctor. Don't prescribe until you've diagnosed.

The gap between where they are and where they want to be = their WHY to buy.

---

### Bucket 1: Current State (Pain)

> "So tell me — what's going on right now? What made you fill out the form?"  
> "Walk me through your average day. What are you spending most of your time on?"  
> "When did it get to this point?"  
> "What's the thing that's eating the most of your time right now?"  
> "Is it just you doing all of this, or do you have some help?"

**What you're listening for:** Tasks they hate, time they're losing, stress, revenue stuck.

---

### Bucket 2: Desired State (Dream)

> "If you had a VA handling all of that — what would you actually be doing with your time?"  
> "Where do you want the business to be in 6 months if this actually gets solved?"  
> "What does that life look like for you?"  
> "Why 6 figures? What does that number actually mean for you?"

**What you're listening for:** Their vision, their WHY, the emotional payoff.

---

### Bucket 3: Cost of Inaction (Reality Check)

> "So you've been in this mode for how long?"  
> "What happens if nothing changes in the next 6 months?"  
> "What's it costing you — in time, in stress, in missed revenue — to stay stuck here?"  
> "If you're at $10K now and you want $25K, but nothing changes operationally — how does that math work?"

**What you're listening for:** The pain of staying still. This builds urgency without fake scarcity.

---

### Pre-Empting Objections During Discovery

Clock the language they use — it tells you what's coming at close.

| What they say | Objection incoming | Pre-empt |
|---|---|---|
| "Things are tight right now" | "Too expensive" | "Just so you know — this is a real investment. Are you at a place where you can commit to that?" |
| "I've been meaning to hire for 2 years" | "Let me think about it" | "What's held you back from doing this already?" |
| "I'd have to run it by my partner" | "Need to talk to spouse" | "What does your partner think about where the business is right now? Would they be on board with something like this?" |

## Phase 3: Presenting the Investment

**Only do this after discovery is done. If they're not open, you're not ready.**

### Micro-Commitment First

Before showing price, get a smaller commitment:

> "Out of everything we talked about — what are the two things that resonated most?"  
> "Do you feel like you're actually at a place where you're ready to make a real change here?"

If they say yes → move to price.

---

### The Price Reveal

**Rules:**
1. Say INVESTMENT, never "cost" or "price"
2. Add "just" to reduce psychological weight
3. Tie it back to their goal
4. Say it once, then SHUT UP

> "Okay, so the initial investment to get your VA placed and the automations running is just going to be $1,600 a month."

Then silence.

First person to speak after price loses.

**Energy:** Starbucks barista. Calm, casual, confident. Not asking for permission.

---

### A/B Close (if offering options)

> "We have two options — A is [X], B is [Y]. Which one makes more sense for where you're at right now?"

No option to say no. It's yes or yes.

## Objection Handling — 4-Step Framework

**Step 1: ACKNOWLEDGE** — don't agree, just defuse  
**Step 2: ASK** — get context, find the real objection  
**Step 3: ANSWER** — reframe with logic + story  
**Step 4: RECLOSE** — bring it back to a yes/no

---

### "It's Too Expensive"

**Acknowledge:** "I hear you. I get it."

**Ask:** "When you say expensive — is it the monthly number, or is it more about not being sure it's worth it yet?"

**Answer:**
> "Can I challenge something? There's a difference between the price and the cost. The price is $1,600/month. The cost is what you're paying right now by doing this all yourself — in time, in stress, in the deals you're not closing because you're stuck in ops. You told me you're losing X hours a week. At your hourly value, that's [do the math]. The investment is $1,600. The cost of doing nothing is way more than that."

**Reclose:** "So knowing that — does the investment still feel like the bigger number?"

---

### "I Need to Think About It"

**Acknowledge:** "Yeah, totally. Makes sense."

**Ask:** "Just so I understand — when you say think about it, is it more that you need more information, or is there something specific that's making you hesitate?"

**Answer:**
> "You told me you've been in this situation for [X months/years]. What changes if you think about it for another week? The ops are still on your plate. The growth is still stalled. The thing that changes is the time you've already lost. What would actually need to be true for you to feel ready?"

**Reclose:** "If I answered that right now, would you be ready to move forward?"

---

### "I Need to Talk to My Spouse/Partner"

**Acknowledge:** "Of course."

**Ask:** "Just so I can help you with that conversation — when you've made business investments before, was it joint or more your call? What do you think their main concern would be?"

**Listen:** Often the real objection is price, not the spouse. If they say "he'll think it's expensive" — it's a price objection wearing a costume.

**Answer (if it's really price):**
> "So it sounds like the main thing is whether it makes financial sense. That's fair. Let me ask — if we could structure the payment to make it easier on cash flow, does that help? And also — does your partner feel the pain of the business being stuck, or is it mostly you?"

**Answer (if it's genuinely joint):**
> "That totally makes sense. What I'd say is — get on the same page with him and let me know. The one thing I'd mention is that the sooner we get you started, the sooner you see results, and the results tend to speak for themselves. Can you commit to talking to him today?"

**Reclose:** "What would you need from that conversation to feel good about moving forward?"

---

### "I Can't Afford It Right Now"

**Acknowledge:** "I hear you. Cash flow is real."

**Ask:** "Is it that the number is bigger than what you have available right now, or is it more about not being sure it's the right move?"

**Answer (cash flow issue):**
> "Okay. So if we structured it in a way that felt lighter on cash flow upfront — does that change the conversation? And also — your revenue is at $X. If the VA frees you up to close even one more deal a month, that's already more than the investment. Is the risk really the $1,600, or is it the uncertainty of whether it'll work?"

**Reclose:** "If we made the first payment easier, would you be ready to move forward?"

---

### "What If It Doesn't Work Out?"

**Acknowledge:** "That's a real concern."

**Ask:** "What specifically are you worried about — the VA not being a fit, or the results not being there?"

**Answer:**
> "Here's how we handle that. We match you based on your specific needs, we do a 2-week onboarding, and we replace the VA if it's not the right fit. We're not just sending you a random hire and ghosting you — this is fully managed. And I'll be honest — the clients who don't see results are usually the ones who don't actually hand things off. If you commit to it, the results show up. Do you feel like you're actually ready to delegate?"

**Reclose:** "Knowing we back you up — does that help?"

## Post-Close Rules

**If they say YES:**
- Move straight to paperwork and onboarding
- Do NOT keep selling or listing more benefits
- Do NOT get insecure and re-explain everything
- Say: "Perfect. Let's get you set up. Here's what happens next."

**If they ask a question:** Answer it, redirect to paperwork.

**If new objection comes up:** 4-step again, then reclose.

---

## Mindset Principles (For George + Shemily)

1. Objections are not rejection. They're requests for more information.
2. The sale happens at 80% — in discovery. If you're losing at close, the problem is in the middle of the call, not the end.
3. Energy is contagious. If you're low energy, the prospect catches it.
4. When you lose a deal, ask: where did the gap open? Opening? Discovery? Price? Handle that, not the symptom.
5. Preserve energy between calls — don't spiral after a no. Add a tally, reset, move.
6. Personal discipline = sales performance. Make your bed. Eat. Sleep. The confidence is built before the call starts.
