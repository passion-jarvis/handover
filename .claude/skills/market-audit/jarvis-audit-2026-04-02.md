## Executive Summary

Jarvis scores 51 out of 100 — below average. The business has real substance: a strong vetting story (1 in 200), a 14-day money-back guarantee, seven documented case studies with real metrics, and a price point that undercuts US-based competitors by 40-60%. These are genuine advantages. The problem is that the marketing doesn't deploy them effectively.

The biggest gap is visibility. Jarvis is functionally invisible in organic search — zero indexed keyword rankings, no pricing page, a blog that hasn't published in 7 months, and thin industry landing pages that Google won't rank. The entire inbound operation runs on Meta ads, which means the moment CPMs rise or the account gets flagged, growth stops. That's a single point of failure for a company trying to 5x its client base.

The second biggest gap is conversion leakage. The homepage has three competing CTAs with no hierarchy, no pricing anchor, and no urgency. Qualified buyers land on the site, can't figure out what it costs, get confused by conflicting actions, and leave. The hire form doesn't connect to a booking calendar, creating a dead zone where leads die. Fixing these conversion issues requires no new ad spend — just changes to existing pages.

The third gap is brand authority. There's no visible founder, no named team, and inconsistent stats (97% vs 98% accuracy, "200+ founders" vs 22 active clients) that erode credibility with sharp buyers. Passion's personal brand — 111K followers of the exact ICP — is completely disconnected from Jarvis.

The three moves with the highest leverage right now: (1) email the 42 warm leads immediately with a 5-part sequence, (2) collapse CTAs to one primary action and add a pricing anchor to the hire page, and (3) put Passion's face and story on the brand. Combined revenue impact estimate: $15,000-35,000 in additional MRR within 90 days without increasing ad spend.
